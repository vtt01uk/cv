Facts

  1. Carver left home at the age of 12 to seek an education. By 1890, he was studying music and art at Simpson College; he became an accomplished painter, exhibiting his art at the 1893 World’s Fair.

  2. Deciding to pursue horticulture, Carver became the first African-American to enroll at what is today Iowa State University. Upon graduation he became the school’s first African-American faculty member.

  3. In 1896, Booker T. Washington asked Carver to become the director of the Agriculture Department at Tuskegee Normal and Industrial School in Tuskegee, Ala. His career in Alabama lasted for five decades.
